WHAT IS DUNGEONS & DRAGONS?

D&D is a tabletop roleplaying game first published by Wizards of the Coast in 1977, and has since evolved into its current 5th edition (5e).

The game consists on playing an adventure through the world that a Dungeon Master presents to the players. You'll explore worlds filled with magic and exciting danger, and hopefully have fun along the way. Whether you derive passion from insightful banter or bone-crushing combat, D&D has something in it for you.

DICE

Have you ever wondered what those pretty geometric shapes are used for?

Well, check out this comprehensive guide about the 7 types of dice we use to play Dungeons and Dragons!

DUNGEON MASTER

The dungeon master is the rule lawyer of the game, he controls everything in the world you play in but the players.

Playing the game requires one DM, find out more about the role of the DM here!

PLAYERS

The players are an essential component of D&D, they are the ones to craft the story along with the Dungeon Master.

Playing the game requires at least one player, they control their player character or PC and explore the world the DM presents!

ROLEPLAYING

Roleplaying is the heart of the game, it's playing as your own character in the world you're exploring. Anything from chatting up an NPC (non-player character) at a tavern to exploring ruinous forests!

COMBAT

Some may find the core of the game to be its combat. That's what makes every roleplaying game unique! Test the strength of your party against monsters ranging from kobolds to dragons!

Combat involves the more complex facets of the game, and a lot of dice-rolling! But remember, your fate lies with you, be creative, don't let the dice dictate the course of your game.

ABILITY CHECKS

Ability checks allow you to know how well your character does at a given task, say you want to take down a door or pick a lock? Or maybe you want to talk an NPC out of calling the royal guard? Trying to recall what that plant was named?

All of those and many more require ability checks, which consist on rolling a d20 and adding the relevant modifier.

SAVING THROWS

Saving throws are prompts for a given creature to avoid a hazard they would otherwise be affected by.

Think, for instance, avoiding a trap, or dodging out of the way of a beam of fire. Other types of saving throws may include resisting the effect of spells.

ATTACKS

Attacks are the basic offensive tactic in the game, trying to hit a single enemy with a weapon — be it melee or ranged — or a spell will often require an attack.

When attacking an enemy, you're trying to beat their Armor Class (or AC) in order to hit.

MAGIC ITEMS

You've been crawling through dungeons for hours already and happen to find an odd-looking potion, or maybe a glowing staff?

Magic items are special enchanted artifacts which give you features and abilities beyond a common object's standards.
