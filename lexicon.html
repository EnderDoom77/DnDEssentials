Ability

There are six different abilities in D&D; and they determine your prowess in certain fields, they are the following:

  • Strength (STR)
  • Dexterity (DEX)
  • Constitution (CON)
  • Intelligence (INT)
  • Wisdom (WIS)
  • Charisma (CHA)

Abilities are used to compute nearly every modifier in D&D, and they're the basis of Ability Checks

Abilities are also linked to skills and spellcasting.

AC

AC is an abbreviation of Armor Class.

Acrobatics

Acrobatics is one of the abilities related to a given creature, it is based on Dexterity.

It measures your ability to perform complicated motions swiftly and accurately, like running along a thin ledge or wall-jumping onto a roof.

Armor Class

Armor class (AC) refers to the difficulty associated with hitting a creature.

AC is generally calculated based on Dexterity (your ability to dodge an attack), set by heavy armor (tough to pierce through and deal damage), or otherwise affected by magic equipment or natural armor for certain creatures.

There are also certain bonuses to AC, like that granted by a shield or by spells such as Shield of Faith

Charisma (CHA)

One of six abilities of a creture. It defines their skill in social interactions and their general appeal (both physical and social).

Its related skills are:

  • Deception
  • Intimidation
  • Performance
  • Persuasion

You can learn more about ability scores in relation to your character as well as to gameplay in Ability Checks.

Charisma is the spellcasting ability of Bards, Paladins, Sorcerers, and Warlocks.

CR

Abbreviation of Challenge Rating.

DC

Abbreviation of Difficulty Class.

Difficulty Class

A check's Difficulty Class is a value which must be beat in order for the check to succeed.

Out of three types of d20 checks in the game, two use Difficulty Class — Ability Checks and Saving Throws — and one uses Armor Class — Attacks.

Deception

Deception is one of the abilities related to a given creature, it is based on Charisma.

It revolves around the idea of lying or otherwise obscuring the truth to deceive others and get your way.
