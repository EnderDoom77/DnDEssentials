DICE

Power dice

These dice are the type you'll use to roll for things like damage, healing, or other effects that require a random number for generic intents.

For instance, spells like Fireball require rolling 8d6, meaning 8 six-sided die.

Basic weapon damage is calculated by rolling the die related to the weapon and then adding your Strength or Dexterity modifier. For more information on attacks, check out Attacks.

Some d10s contain a 0 instead of a 10, this result is a 10 and not a 0.

The d20; check die.

The d20 is by far the most used die in the whole game, it's used to calculate every type of check that may result is a failure or success.

It's used for Ability Checks, Saving Throws, and Attacks. In any of these checks, one is trying to beat a given number with the value rolled in the d20 + any relevant modifiers.

You may sometimes be asked to roll with advantage or disadvantage. When rolling with advantage, roll 2d20 and take the value of the highest one. When rolling with disadvantage, roll 2d20 and take the lowest.

Advantage and disadvantage don't stack, so you can only roll up to 2d20. If a roll would be made with both advantage and disadvantage, simply roll as normal.

The d100; percentile dice.

In very few occasions, an event occurs which can lead to many different options, when this happens, roll a d100.

A d100 roll consists of rolling 2d10 where one is marked as the "tens" die and the other is marked as the "units" die. Simply mash the numbers together to obtain your result.

The d10 contains a 0, charts generally contain "00" instead of "100", in either case, rolling two tens is a 100.

How to read dice rolls in rules.

Generally, dice rolling in the rules is expressed in the following format:

[N]d[D](+MOD)

N = number of dice to roll.

d = die type separator.

D = type of die to roll. e.g. A d6 is a 6-sided die.

MOD = a modifier, it's added to the result of the final roll to obtain the total.

A few examples could be:
4d4: Roll 4 four-sided dice and add them together.
1d12 + 4: Roll a twelve-sided die and add four to the result.
2d6 - 3 Roll two six-sided dice and then subtract three from the end result.
